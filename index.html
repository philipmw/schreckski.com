Who

Mike Schreck is a PSIA-certified Cross-Country Level 2 Instructor in Snoqualmie Pass, Washington, an hour east of Seattle by I-90.

Nordic ski lessons will help you to ski faster and more efficiently on your Skate or Classic skis!

Twelve seasons instructing 5 to 80+ year old aspiring cross country skate or classic skiers how to be more efficient in their skiing techniques.

What

Private single or two person lessons. No group lessons with strangers!

Single or multiple week one hour lessons. (2 lessons can be combined into a 2 hour lesson if requested.) Prepay for a 3 hour/ 3 lessons package and get the 4th lesson on me!

On snow, rollerskis or by bike!

Flexible with your schedule, your ability and your passion!

Where

  • Monday and Friday: Hyak Sno-Park. For new to Nordic aspiring cross country skiers wanting flat terrain. Over 10 miles of beautiful groomed rail trail along Kachelus Lake.
  • Wednesday, Thursday and Saturday: Crystal Springs Sno-Park. Many miles of some of the best variety of terrain for beginner to advanced cross country skiers. Also, Lots of Sno Park parking and fewer crowds than the other Snoqualmie area Sno Parks! (NOTE: We meet on the intersection of the Stampede Pass and the Palouse-to-Cascades Trail. You must get yourself to this intersection, as my Commercial Use Permit is with the State Parks. If you are unfamiliar with this area, see Monday/Friday at Hyak Sno-Park above.)
  • Lake Easton or Suncadia Golf Course are possible options if they are groomed and I am not already booked. Call me if you are interested in these locations. All have varied more challenging terrain with hills, curves for XC skiers wanting more challenging terrain. Note: there is no flat teaching terrain. Also I do not teach at Cabin Creek.

Remember, for all groomed Sno Parks, you need a daily ($25) or full season Sno Park pass with special grooming permit ($120/car). Skiing and parking is free at Suncadia!

Also if DOT requires chains for all but AWD, put them on if you aren't driving an AWD vehicle. It's now a $500 fine if caught!
